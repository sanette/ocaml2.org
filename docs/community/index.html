Community

    

        

            
            
        

        

            
OCaml has a diverse, worldwide community and one of the main benefits is the ability to easily reach each other.  Mailing lists and web forums are the most common way people interact although you will always find users on IRC, around the web and at any of the meetings.  OCaml also has a committed group of industrial users who regularly contribute to the community.

Mailing Lists and Web Forums

            

            

                
discuss.ocaml.org

                Main forum

                Very active forum for all users, including
                beginners, to discuss any topic about OCaml.
                It can be subscribed to like a mailing list.

                
caml-list@inria.fr

                Main OCaml List

                Intended for all users of OCaml. The purpose of this list is to share experience, exchange ideas and code, and discuss applications of the language.

            

            

                
caml-announce@inria.fr

                Low volume announcements

                This is a low-traffic, moderated list for announcements of OCaml releases and new OCaml-related software, libraries, documents, etc.

                
lists.ocaml.org

                Community lists and working groups

                User-interest lists and working groups convened with specific goals.

Support

            
See the mailing lists and web forums page for a complete list of support channels. Commercial support is also available.

            
If you would like to give support to OCaml, you can join the Consortium or support the work of OCaml Labs. Find out more.

            
Suggestions or bug reports about the OCaml language
            itself can be posted to Gihub issues.
